# Research Report: Academic Perspectives & Consumer Behavior (2024-2025)
**Date:** 2026-03-03
**Subject:** Analyzing adoption models (UTAUT2) and current "Wallet Fatigue" trends.

---

## 1. Key Finding: The "Wallet Fatigue 2025" Study
*   **Principal Investigator:** DECTA (UK/EU wide survey, 1500+ users, 50k+ reviews).
*   **Core Finding:** Users are suffering from **app-switching fatigue** in personal finance.
*   **Key Statistics:**
    - **60%** of respondents use a *second* finance app solely because their primary app lacks specific features.
    - **91%** of users would **consolidate into a single application** if it met all their needs.
    - **81%** of merchants expect digital wallets/open banking to dominate transaction methods by 2025.

### Top "Missing Features" in Current Apps:
1.  **Multi-Platform Integration (65%):** Users want one app to connect to *all* their banks/accounts.
2.  **Advanced Security Tools (56.2%):** Better fraud detection and identity management.
3.  **Advanced Budgeting/Spending Analysis (35%):** Deeper automated insights, not just raw lists.

## 2. Adoption Framework: The UTAUT2 Model for PFM
Based on Sept 2025/July 2025 research, the **Unified Theory of Acceptance and Use of Technology (UTAUT2)** highlights what drives users to actually *stick* with a finance app:

### Dominant Success Drivers (What matters most):
| Variable | Impact | Definition/Why it Matters |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Performance Expectancy (PE)** | **CRITICAL** | The user's belief that the app saves them money or improves their financial health. |
| **Habit (H)** | **CRITICAL** | Daily or weekly usage routines (frictionless logging is key). |
| **Effort Expectancy (EE)** | **HIGH** | Ease of use. If it requires manual work, adoption fails. |
| **Facilitating Conditions (FC)** | **HIGH** | Availability of support and device compatibility (cross-platform sync). |

### Less Significant Factors:
- **Social Influence:** People don't care much if their friends use the same finance app (it's personal).
- **Price Value:** Users are willing to pay for a tool that saves them more than it costs.

---

## 3. Emerging "Fatigue" Categories (The Financial Brand, 2025)
Beyond "Wallet Fatigue," two new risks are identified for our PFM design to avoid:
1.  **AI Fatigue (Only 17% Very Comfortable):** Users are becoming skeptical of "hallucinating" or generic AI advice. **Recommendation:** Focus on "AI as an assistant," not "AI as the decision-maker."
2.  **Marketing Fatigue:** Users are ignoring product-centric messages. **Recommendation:** Transition to *advice-centric* or *value-centric* messaging.

---

## 4. Strategic Recommendation for fuku
- **Solve the Fragmentation:** Prioritize "The One App" vision (Consolidation) by building robust bank/account import features.
- **Contextual AI:** Use AI only for **high-accuracy tasks** (Categorization, Pattern Detection) rather than unsolicited financial advice, to avoid "AI Fatigue."
- **Focus on Routine:** Build features that encourage daily "micro-habits" (Instant notifications, easy expense logging).
